Building a High School Robotics Project


See how our students designed and built a competition-ready robot, combining mechanics, electronics, and coding.



In our latest session, students tackled building a robot capable of completing multiple tasks autonomously. They learned basic mechanics, motor control, sensor integration, and programming in Python. The project encouraged teamwork, problem-solving, and creative thinking, culminating in a fun mini-competition.


This hands-on approach ensures students don't just learn theory—they experience the engineering process from start to finish, gaining skills they can apply in school competitions, STEM fairs, and beyond.

Bridge Engineering: Student Innovations


Learn how students applied physics and material science to construct model bridges balancing strength and creativity.



Students explored structural engineering concepts by designing bridges with balsa wood and other materials. Each team tested load-bearing capacity, experimented with trusses, and iterated designs to optimize strength.


The exercise emphasized real-world engineering challenges: analyzing data, troubleshooting failures, and learning from mistakes.

Intro to Python for Young Engineers


Discover how students use Python to simulate circuits, control robots, and analyze engineering experiments.



Our coding workshops introduce students to Python in a hands-on, engaging way. They learn to write scripts for simulating experiments, controlling robotic projects, and analyzing engineering data.


This approach not only builds programming skills but reinforces critical thinking and logical problem-solving, essential tools for any aspiring engineer.
